Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Karelian Bear Dogs, our breeding program, and the adoption process.

General Questions

What is a Karelian Bear Dog?

The Karelian Bear Dog is an ancient breed from Northwestern Europe, specifically the Karelian region of Finland and Russia. They are medium-sized spitz-type dogs known for their courage, loyalty, and exceptional hunting abilities. Originally bred to hunt large game including bears, moose, wolves, and lynx, they are fearless, intelligent, and make excellent working dogs and family companions when properly trained.

How big do Karelian Bear Dogs get?

Males typically stand 21-24 inches tall at the shoulder and weigh 55-61 pounds. Females are slightly smaller, measuring 19-22 inches in height and weighing 37-44 pounds. They have a robust, muscular build that is strong without being heavy, with males being noticeably larger than females.

What is their temperament like?

Karelian Bear Dogs have a courageous, persistent, and self-confident temperament. They are highly devoted to their families and make excellent guard dogs, being naturally wary of strangers. They are intelligent and independent thinkers, which can make training challenging for inexperienced owners. With proper socialization, they are stable and reliable companions who are never aggressive toward people but will protect their family when necessary.

Are they good with children?

Karelian Bear Dogs can be good with children, especially when raised with them from puppyhood. They are generally patient and protective of family members. However, due to their high energy levels and strong prey drive, they may play too roughly with small children. We recommend them for families with older children who understand how to interact respectfully with dogs. Early socialization and training are essential for creating a safe environment for both children and the dog.

Health & Care

What health issues are common in Karelian Bear Dogs?

Karelian Bear Dogs are generally a healthy breed with fewer genetic issues than many purebred dogs. However, they can be susceptible to certain conditions including hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, eye problems like cataracts, and dental issues. Responsible breeding practices, including health testing of parent dogs, helps minimize these risks. Regular veterinary check-ups, proper nutrition, and maintaining a healthy weight are important for preventing health problems.

How long do Karelian Bear Dogs live?

The average lifespan of a Karelian Bear Dog is 10-13 years with proper care. Factors that contribute to longevity include quality nutrition, regular exercise, routine veterinary care, and maintaining a healthy weight. Dogs from responsible breeders who perform health testing on parent dogs typically have better long-term health outcomes.

How much exercise do they need?

Karelian Bear Dogs are high-energy dogs that require 1-2 hours of vigorous exercise daily. This should include long walks, running, hiking, or engaging activities like agility training. They excel when they have a job to do and need both physical and mental stimulation. Without adequate exercise, they can become destructive or develop behavioral problems. They are not suitable for sedentary families or apartment living.

What should I feed my Karelian Bear Dog?

Feed your Karelian Bear Dog high-quality dog food appropriate for their age, size, and activity level. As an active, working breed, they may benefit from performance or active breed formulas that provide adequate protein and energy. Feed measured meals twice daily rather than free-feeding to maintain a healthy weight. Always provide fresh water. Consult with your veterinarian about specific nutritional needs, especially for growing puppies and senior dogs.

How much grooming do they require?

Karelian Bear Dogs have a dense double coat that requires weekly brushing to remove loose fur and prevent matting. They shed moderately year-round with heavier shedding during seasonal changes. During shedding periods, more frequent brushing may be needed. They only need occasional baths unless they get particularly dirty. Regular nail trimming, ear cleaning, and dental care are also important parts of their grooming routine.

Training & Behavior

Are Karelian Bear Dogs easy to train?

Karelian Bear Dogs are intelligent and capable of learning quickly, but they can be challenging to train due to their independent nature. They are not recommended for first-time dog owners. Training requires consistency, patience, and positive reinforcement methods. They respond best to confident handlers who can provide strong leadership without harsh punishment. Early socialization and puppy training classes are essential for developing good behavior.

Do they get along with other pets?

Karelian Bear Dogs typically do not get along well with other pets, especially smaller animals, due to their strong prey drive. They can be aggressive toward other dogs, particularly those of the same sex. While some individuals can learn to coexist with other pets if raised together from puppyhood, they generally do best as the only pet in the household. Early socialization is crucial, but their natural instincts may still create challenges in multi-pet homes.

How much do they bark?

Karelian Bear Dogs are naturally vocal and were bred to bark when they locate game. They tend to bark at anything they're unsure of, including unfamiliar people and animals. This makes them excellent watchdogs but can be problematic in urban or suburban settings. Training can help manage excessive barking, but it's important to understand that barking is a natural behavior for this breed. They're best suited for rural or country settings where their vocal nature is less likely to cause issues.

Can they be left alone during the day?

Karelian Bear Dogs form strong bonds with their families and prefer not to be left alone for long periods. They can develop separation anxiety or destructive behaviors if left alone too frequently. While adult dogs can handle being alone for reasonable periods (4-6 hours) if properly exercised and mentally stimulated, they thrive in homes where someone is present most of the day. They're not suited for families with long work hours unless arrangements can be made for exercise and companionship.

Our Breeding Program

How long have you been breeding Karelian Bear Dogs?

Kami Kennels has been dedicated to breeding exceptional Karelian Bear Dogs for over 15 years. Our experience spans multiple generations of dogs, allowing us to develop a deep understanding of the breed's characteristics, health considerations, and temperament traits. We work closely with other reputable breeders and breed organizations to maintain the highest standards in our breeding program.

What health testing do you do on parent dogs?

All our breeding dogs undergo comprehensive health testing before being included in our program. This includes hip and elbow dysplasia screening through OFA or PennHIP, eye examinations by a veterinary ophthalmologist, and genetic testing for known hereditary conditions. We also conduct general health screenings and ensure all breeding dogs are up to date on vaccinations and preventive care. All test results are available to prospective puppy buyers.

How do you socialize your puppies?

Our puppies receive extensive socialization from birth through adoption. They're raised in our home environment where they experience normal household sounds, activities, and interactions. We follow the Puppy Culture program, which includes early neurological stimulation, problem-solving exercises, and exposure to various surfaces, sounds, and experiences. Puppies interact with people of all ages, other dogs, and are introduced to different environments to build confidence and adaptability.

What comes with your puppies?

All our puppies come with a comprehensive package including: AKC registration papers, complete health records with vaccination and deworming history, a 2-year health guarantee against genetic defects, a starter supply of high-quality puppy food, a comfort blanket with mother's scent, basic training materials, and lifetime support from our team. We also provide detailed care instructions and are always available for questions and guidance throughout your dog's life.

Do you offer health guarantees?

Yes, we provide a comprehensive 2-year health guarantee covering genetic and congenital defects. If a puppy develops a covered health issue, we will work with you to find the best solution, which may include replacement or refund depending on the circumstances. This guarantee demonstrates our commitment to producing healthy puppies and our confidence in our breeding program. We also require that puppies receive regular veterinary care to maintain the guarantee.

Adoption Process

How do I reserve a puppy?

To reserve a puppy, you first need to complete our adoption application and be approved. Once approved, we require a deposit to hold your chosen puppy. Deposits are typically $500 and are applied toward the total purchase price. We maintain a waiting list for upcoming litters, and deposits are placed in order of receipt. All deposits are refundable if we cannot provide a puppy within a reasonable timeframe or if the chosen puppy doesn't pass our health screening.

What is your adoption application process?

Our adoption process includes: 1) Initial contact to discuss your interest and experience, 2) Completion of our detailed application covering your living situation, dog experience, and plans for the puppy, 3) Reference checks including your veterinarian, 4) Phone or in-person interview, 5) Home visit if feasible, 6) Approval and puppy selection. This thorough process ensures our puppies go to appropriate homes and helps us match the right puppy with the right family.

Can I visit your facility?

Yes, we welcome visits from serious, pre-approved prospective buyers. Due to biosecurity concerns and to minimize stress on our dogs, we don't allow casual visits. Approved visitors can meet our adult dogs, see our facilities, and interact with available puppies. We also conduct virtual meetings and provide extensive photos and videos for those who cannot visit in person. All visits are by appointment only and follow strict protocols to protect the health of our dogs.

Do you ship puppies?

We prefer that families pick up their puppies in person to ensure a good match and provide proper transition guidance. However, we understand that distance can be a challenge and will work with approved families to arrange transportation. We can recommend professional pet transport services or arrange for puppies to be flown with a companion. All shipping costs are the responsibility of the buyer, and we ensure all travel arrangements prioritize the puppy's safety and comfort.

What if I can no longer care for my dog?

We require in our contract that if you cannot keep your dog for any reason, you must return it to us. We will take back any dog we've bred at any point in their life, regardless of the reason. We then work to find a new, appropriate home for the dog. This lifetime commitment ensures that none of our puppies ever end up in shelters or inappropriate situations. We also provide support and resources to help families work through challenges before considering rehoming.

Pricing & Availability

How much do your puppies cost?

Our puppy prices typically range from $2,500 to $3,500 depending on the specific litter, parentage, and individual puppy qualities. Show-quality puppies or those with exceptional markings may be priced higher. This price includes all health testing, vaccinations, microchipping, registration papers, health guarantee, and lifetime support. We require a $500 deposit to reserve a puppy, with the balance due at pickup. Payment plans may be available for qualified buyers.

When will you have puppies available?

We typically have 2-3 litters per year, with puppies available in spring, summer, and fall. Our breeding schedule is planned carefully to ensure the health of our mothers and quality of each litter. We maintain a waiting list for interested families and announce upcoming litters to those on our list first. The best way to stay informed about availability is to join our waiting list and follow our website and social media for updates.

Do you offer payment plans?

We understand that purchasing a quality purebred dog is a significant investment. For qualified, approved buyers, we may offer payment plans with reasonable terms. Typically, this involves a deposit to reserve the puppy, followed by monthly payments until the puppy is ready to go home. The full balance must be paid before the puppy leaves our facility. We work with each family individually to find arrangements that work within their budget while ensuring our puppies go to stable, prepared homes.
